# Vowels

Nyrakai has a rich vowel system with 20 vowel sounds: 5 short, 5 long, and 10 diphthongs.

---

## Short Vowels (5)

| Letter | IPA | Sound | Example |
|--------|-----|-------|---------|
| a | /a/ | "father" | tal (say) |
| e | /e/ | "bed" | sem (good) |
| i | /i/ | "see" | gīn (seed) |
| o | /o/ | "go" | pol (round) |
| u | /u/ | "too" | yun (do) |

---

## Long Vowels (5)

Long vowels are marked with a macron (¯) and held for approximately twice the duration.

| Letter | IPA | Sound | Example |
|--------|-----|-------|---------|
| ā | /aː/ | long "father" | gān (tree) |
| ē | /eː/ | long "bed" | țēl (ear) |
| ī | /iː/ | long "see" | yīr (eye) |
| ō | /oː/ | long "go" | gōr (dog) |
| ū | /uː/ | long "too" | țūn (drink) |

---

## Short Diphthongs (5)

Diphthongs are vowel combinations pronounced as a single syllable.

| Letter | IPA | Sound | Example |
|--------|-----|-------|---------|
| æ | /ai/ | "eye" | kæ (tongue) |
| ɒ | /au/ | "cow" | šɒ (that) |
| ɛ | /ei/ | "day" | t'ɛm (eat) |
| ə | /eu/ | "ay-oo" | nəl (cloud) |
| œ | /oi/ | "boy" | šœ (this) |

---

## Long Diphthongs (5)

Long diphthongs extend the first vowel sound.

| Letter | IPA | Sound | Example |
|--------|-----|-------|---------|
| ǣ | /aːi/ | long "eye" | sǣl (oil) |
| ɒ̄ | /aːu/ | long "cow" | nɒ̄k (nose) |
| ɛ̄ | /eːi/ | long "day" | sɛ̄l (ear) |
| ə̄ | /eːu/ | long "ay-oo" | fə̄n (foot) |
| œ̄ | /oːi/ | long "boy" | šœ̄k (head) |

---

## Glottal Modified Vowels

The apostrophe (') before a vowel adds a schwa + glottal stop sound (/əʔ/).

| Pattern | IPA | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| 'V | /əʔV/ | n'æ (true) → /nəʔai/ |

This creates a brief "catch" in the throat before the vowel, giving words a distinctive ancient quality.
